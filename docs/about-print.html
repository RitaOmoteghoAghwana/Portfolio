01 — The Story

How Dreams
became Diamonds.

Five years ago, I built my first WordPress site for a friend's hair brand at midnight, on a borrowed laptop, fueled by the kind of stubborn optimism only first-time designers have. The site shipped. The brand grew. And I realized something: most people aren't short on dreams — they're short on someone who can shape them into something tangible.

That's the job, really. Taking the half-formed vision in someone's head — the brand they keep meaning to launch, the song they want to release, the business they keep promising to take seriously — and turning it into pixels, frames, and pages that actually do the work.

Today I work with founders, musicians, coaches and brands across Fiverr, LinkedIn and direct referrals. I've shipped over 120 projects, helped indie artists clear hundreds of thousands of streams with AI-generated music videos, and built WordPress sites that load fast, look expensive and convert quietly in the background.

The tagline "From Dreams to Diamonds" isn't marketing fluff. It's the actual through-line. Every project starts rough. The work is in the pressure, the polish, and the patience.

If that sounds like the kind of partner you've been looking for — let's talk.

04 — Principles

Three rules I won't break.

PRINCIPLE / 01

Ship the real work, not a demo.

Polished portfolios are easy. Sites that convert, videos that move people, and content that ships on time — that's the actual job.

PRINCIPLE / 02

Make it feel like you, not like a template.

I use Elementor and WordPress, but no two sites I make look the same. Your brand's quirks are the feature, not the bug.

PRINCIPLE / 03

Tell me the truth, get the truth back.

No client-pleasing nods. If your copy is weak or your idea won't convert, I'll say so — and offer a better version.
